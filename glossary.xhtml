Glossary

The Accelerator

The Accelerator

A parameter that defines the relationship between national income and required capital stock.

Aggregation

Aggregation

Taking a 'big picture' view of economic activity adding up all observed values of a variable into a single measure.

An Asset

An Asset

Anything of value owned by an individual, institution or economic agent.

Autonomous Expenditure

Autonomous Expenditure

Expenditure that takes place independent of national income.

A Bond

A Bond

A long term (10+ years) debt instrument.

Business Inventories

Business Inventories

Additions or deletions to existing inventory levels in response to economic conditions (a flow variable).

Business Cycle

Business Cycle

An economic contraction (recession) followed by an expansion.

Capital Gain

Capital Gain

A positive difference between the sale price of an asset and its purchase price.

Capital Loss

Capital Loss

A negative difference between the sale price of an asset and its purchase price.

Complete Preferences

Complete Preferences

The ability of a consumer to fully identify his/her preference for any combination or bundle of goods and services.

Constant Returns to Scale (CRS)

Constant Returns to Scale

A long run production concept where a doubling of all factor inputs exactly doubles the amount of output.

Consumer

Consumer

An economic agent that desires to purchase goods and services with the goal of maximizing the satisfaction (utility) from consumption of those goods and services.

Consumer Optimum

Consumer Optimum

Identification of an attainable bundle of goods that maximizes a consumer's level of satisfaction given his/her level of income and market prices.

Consumer Price Index (CPI)

Consumer Price Index

A weighted average of the prices of a representative market basket of goods and services that represents consumption patterns in some base time period.

Cyclical Unemployment

Cyclical Unemployment

Changes in unemployment attributed to cyclical behavior in economic activity.

Deflation

Deflation

A decline in the aggregate price level over some defined time period.

Demand

Demand

A relationship between market price and quantities of goods and services purchased in a given period of time.

Depreciation

Depreciation

A measure of the wear and tear that affects capital equipment or other intermediate goods.

Diminishing Marginal Utility

Diminishing Marginal Utility

An economic concept that refers to the notion that additional units consumed of a particular commodity provide less and less additional satisfaction relative to previous units consumed.

Diminishing Marginal Productivity (DMP)

Diminishing Marginal Productivity

A short run production concept where increases in the variable factor of production lead to less and less additional output.

Direct Finance

Direct Finance

The transfer of loanable funds through the use of capital markets (i.e., the Stock and Bond markets) usually facilitated by investment banks.

Disinflation

Disinflation

A decline in the overall rate of inflation. Prices are still rising but by a smaller amount relative to previous time periods.

Disintermediation

Disintermediation

The removal of funds from a financial intermediary (a bank or other depository institution).

Disposable Personal Income

Disposable Personal Income

Personal Income less taxes paid.

Durable Goods

Durable Goods

Goods that deliver consumption services over an extended period of time.

Economic Expansion

Economic Expansion

Growth in Real GDP for one fiscal quarter or more.

Economics

Economics

The study of how a given society allocates scarce resources to meet (or satisfy) the unlimited wants and need of its members.

Employment

Employment

A measure of those individuals in the labor force working, at least one hour per week, for pay.

Equilibrium

Equilibrium

A situation where there is no tendency for change.

Exchange Rate

Exchange Rate

The value of a domestic currency expressed in terms of a foreign currency or basket of foreign currencies.

Factors of Production

Factors of Production

An exhaustive list of inputs required for any type of production.

Financial Intermediation

Financial Intermediation

A form of indirect finance where an institution (a bank) acts as an intermediary to reduce transactions costs and facilitate borrowing and lending.

Final Goods and Services

Final Goods and Services

Goods and services that are purchased for direct consumption.

Fixed nonresidential Investment

Fixed nonresidential Investment

Additions to the existing stock of plant and equipment used in the production of goods and services.

Fixed Residential Investment

Fixed Residential Investment

Additions to the existing stock of housing used to provide housing services.

Flow Variable

Flow Variable

A variable that is measured per unit of time.

Frictional Unemployment

Frictional Unemployment

Unemployment that exists as a natural consequence of market activity where individuals are in-between jobs.

GDP

GDP

Gross Domestic Product: The market value of all final goods and services produced in a given time period.

Gross Investment

Gross Investment

Investment that includes additions to the capital stock as well a the replacement of depreciated capital.

Human Capital/Wealth

Human Capital - Wealth

A measure of the skills, ability or productivity of human beings.

Implicit Price Deflator (IPD)

Implicit Price Deflator

The ratio between Nominal GDP and Real GDP.

Income Effect

Income Effect

A reaction of consumer's demand for goods or services due to changes in purchasing power holding relative prices constant (see Substitution Effect).

Income Producing Asset

Income Producing Asset

An asset that is used to generate revenue from the production and sale of goods and services.

Indifference Curve

Indifference Curve

A set of points that represent different bundles of goods which provide the consumer with the same level of satisfaction (or utility).

Indirect Business Taxes

Indirect Business Taxes

Taxes that tend to be built into the price of a particular good (i.e., excise taxes).

Income Taxes

Income Taxes

Taxes that are based on and vary with personal or corporate income.

Indirect Finance

Indirect Finance

The transfer of loanable funds (deposits) through the use of financial intermediaries (commercial banks).

Induced Expenditure

Induced Expenditure

Changes in spending due to changes in (national) income. See the Marginal Propensity to Spend.

Inflation

Inflation

An increase in the price level over some defined time period.

Inferior Good

Inferior Good

A good where quantity demanded decreases when consumer income increases (there is an inverse relationship between quantity demanded and income).

Interest Sensitivity of Investment

Interest Sensitivity of Investment

A measure of responsiveness of investment expenditure to changes to the (real) interest rate.

Interest Sensitivity of Money Demand

Interest Sensitivity of Money Demand

A measure of responsiveness of the demand for cash balances to changes in the (nominal) interest rate.

Intermediate Goods and Services

Intermediate Goods and Services

Goods (or services) used to produce other goods (i.e., capital equipment).

Investment

Investment

Changes to the existing capital stock or business inventories.

Labor Force Participation Rate

Labor Force Participation Rate

The ratio of those in the labor force (the employed and unemployed) and those that are available for work.

Laspeyres Index

Laspeyres Index

A weighted average of prices based on the use of base-period consumption patterns. Also known as the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

Liquidity

Liquidity

A measure of the ease by which a financial asset can be converted into a form readily accepted as payment for goods and services.

Liquidity Premium

Liquidity Premium

An adjustment to a real interest rate to compensate for the direct relationship between uncertainty and the duration of a debt contract.

M1

M1

A narrow money supply measure that includes currency in circulation and the value of demand deposits.

M2

M2

A broad money supply measure that includes currency, demand deposits, and the value of time deposits.

Marginal Propensity to Consume

Marginal Propensity to Consume (MPC)

The fraction of each additional dollar of income devoted to consumption expenditure.

Marginal Propensity to Save (MPS)

Marginal Propensity to Save

The fraction of each additional dollar of income devoted to any type of spending (i.e., consumption, investment, government, or net exports).

Marginal Propensity to Spend

Marginal Propensity to Spend

The fraction of each additional dollar of income devoted to any type of spending (i.e., consumption, investment, government, or net exports).

Marginal Utility

Marginal Utility

The satisfaction a consumer receives by consuming one more unit of some good or service.

Market

Market

A place or institution where buyers and sellers come together and exchange factor inputs or final goods and services. A market is one of several types of economic rationing systems.

Money Market Instrument

Money Market Instrument

A short term (less than 10 years) debt instrument.

Money Multiplier

Money Multiplier

The relationship between changes in the monetary base and the money supply.

Monetary Base

Monetary Base

Also known as High-powered Money. Reserves + Currency in the monetary system -- the main liabilities of the central bank.

National Income

National Income

The sum of all types of income (wages, net interest, profits, and net rental income) earned in a given time period by any type of economic agent (individuals or corporation).

Natural Rate of Unemployment

Natural Rate of Unemployment

That rate of unemployment where there is neither upward nor downward pressure on prices.

Net Investment

Net Investment

Investment exclusive of replacement of depreciated capital.

Nominal GDP

Nominal GDP

GDP measured at current prices.

Nominal Interest Rate

Nominal Interest Rate

The interest rate published as part of a debt contract.

Non-Durable Goods

Non-Durable Goods

Goods that tend to be immediately consumed or deliver consumption services over a short period of time.

Non-Income Producing Asset

Non-Income Producing Asset

Something of value that does not generate any income or revenue stream.

Normal (Current) Yield

Normal (Current) Yield

The ratio between the annual income generated by an asset and its purchase price. Also known as the present value of a perpetuity.

Normal Good

Normal Good

A good where quantity demanded increases when consumer income increases (a direct relationship between quantity demanded and income).

Opportunity Costs

Opportunity Costs

The value of a resource applied to its next best use.

Paasche Index

Paasche Index

A weighted average of prices based on current expenditure patterns. Also known as the GDP (or Implicit Price) Deflator.

Peak

Peak

A point of transition in the business cycle from expansion to contraction.

Permanent Income

Permanent Income

Expected levels of individual income that guide consumption expenditure decisions.

Personal Income

Personal Income

The income earned by individual households in a given time period.

the Phillips Curve

the Phillips Curve

A theoretical relationship between the unemployment rate of a given economy and rates of (wage) inflation.

Potential Output

Potential Output

A measure of the economy's ability to produce goods and services.

Present Value

Present Value

The value of a future payment or stream of payments discounted by some appropriate rate of interest.

Primary Stock/Bond Market

Primary Stock-Bond Market

The market where new shares of stock or new bonds are bought and sold. Activity in this market represents direct finance where actual borrowing and lending activity takes place.

Producer

Producer

An economic agent that converts inputs (factors of production) into output (goods and services) with the goal of maximizing profits from production and sale of those goods and services.

Producer Optimum

Producer Optimum

A choice of input combinations or output levels that maximize the profits of a producer taking all prices as a given.

Profits

Profits

The difference between sales revenue and the costs of production.

The Quantity Equation

The Quantity Equation

Also known as the Equation of Exchange, an identity relating the amount of money in circulation to the price level and level of outputin an aggregate economy.

Rate of Time Preference

Rate of Time Preference

The equivalent of a personal interest (or discount) rate. The measure by which individuals compare current and future economic activity.

Real GDP

Real GDP

GDP measured at constant (some base period) prices.

Real Interest Rate

Real Interest Rate

An interest rate that has been adjusted for changes in the price level or changes in purchasing power over some time period.

Recession

Recession

Negative growth in Real GDP for two or more fiscal quarters.

Relative Price

Relative Price

A ratio of any two prices or one particular price compared to a price index.

Risk

Risk

A measure of uncertainty about the value of an asset or the benefits of some economic activity.

Risk Premium

Risk Premium

An adjustment to a real interest rate to compensate for uncertainty in the ability of a borrower to service a loan.

Satiation

Satiation

A level of consumption where the consumer is fully satisfied in a given period of time.

Savings

Savings

The difference between income and expenditure in the current time period.

Scarcity

Scarcity

A physical or economic condition where the quantity desired of a good or service exceeds the availability of that good or service in the absence of a rationing system.

Services

Services

A consumption category that includes: health services, educational services, architectural services, lega services and so on. Typically these expenditure items can not be invetoried or stored and are produced largely based on the human capital of the prodvider.

Stagnation

Stagnation

An economic condition where an economy is facing relatively high rates of inflation, little or no growth, and high unemployment.

Secondary Stock/Bond Market

Secondary Stock-Bond Market

The market where existing shares of stock or existing bonds are traded. This market provides liquidity to these types of financial assets.

A Share of Stock

A Share of Stock

A financial instrument that give the holder a share of ownership in a publicly held corporation.

Shortage

Shortage

A market condition where the quantity demanded of a particular good or service exceed the quantity available.

Speculation

Speculation

The purchase of a good or asset not intended for final consumption but rather in the expectation of future sale at some higher price.

Spending Multiplier

Spending Multiplier

The relationship between an autonomous spending shock and eventual changes in aggregate income.

Standard of Living

Standard of Living

The ratio of the output of an economy and population. Also known as per-capita output.

Stock Variable

Stock variable

A variable measured at point in time.

Structural Unemployment

Structural Unemployment

Unemployment that exists as a consequence of structural changes in economic activity.

Substitution Effect

Substitution Effect

The reaction of a consumer's demand for goods based on changes in relative prices holding purchasing power (or utility) constant (see Income Effect).

Supply

Supply

A relationship between market price and quantities of goods and services made available for sale in a given period of time.

Surplus

Surplus

A market condition where the quantity supplied exceeds the quantity demanded.

Transitive Preferences

Transitive Preferences

A logical pattern of preferences where preference of one good over a second good and preference of the second good over a third good imply preference for the first good compared to the third good.

Transitory Income

Transitory Income

Unexpected changes or shocks to individual income. Often measured as the difference between observed income and permanent income.

Trough

Trough

A point of transition in the business cycle from contraction to expansion.

Unemployment

Unemployment

The difference between the number of people in the labor force and those working for pay.

Utility

Utility

A measure of the satisfaction received from some type of economic activity (i.e., consumption of goods and services or the sale of factor services).

Velocity

Velocity

The number of times a given quantity (stock) of money changes hands in a given time period (the ratio of expenditure in that time period to a given measure of the money supply).

Yield

Yield

The ratio between the flow of returns (income, revenue, profits) generated by an asset and the purchase price of that asset.

Abundance

Abundance

A physical or economic condition where the quantity available of a resource exceeds the quantity desired in the absence of a rationing system.

Budget Set

Budget Set

Different bundles of goods and services that are attainable to the consumer at given market prices and the consumer's fixed level of income.

Competition

Competition

The process of consumers bidding prices upwards or producers cutting prices in order to allow those agents to be involved in a market trade.

Complementary Goods

Complementary Goods

-A pair of goods where the quantity demanded of one increases when the price of a related good decreases.

Complete Preferences

Complete Preferences

The ability of a consumer to fully identify his/her preference for any combination or bundle of goods and services.

Constant Returns to Scale (CRS)

Constant Returns to Scale (CRS)

A long run production concept where a doubling of all factor inputs exactly doubles the amount of output.

Consumer (household)

Consumer (household)

An economic agent that desires to purchase goods and services with the goal of maximizing the satisfaction from consumption of those goods and services.

Consumer Optimum

Consumer Optimum

Identification of an attainable bundle of goods that maximizes a consumer's level of satisfaction given his/her level of income and market prices.

Consumer's Surplus

Consumer's Surplus

The difference between what a consumer is willing to pay for each unit of a commodity consumed and the price actually paid.

Cross-Price Elasticity of Demand

Cross-Price Elasticity of Demand

A measure of sensitivity in the quantity demanded of one goods in reaction to changes in the price of a related good.

Decreasing Returns to Scale (DRS)

Decreasing Returns to Scale (DRS)

A long run production concept where a doubling of all factor inputs results in less than double the amount of output.

Demand

Demand

A relationship between market price and quantities of goods and services purchased in a given period of time. Represents the behavior of buyers in the market place.

Diminishing Marginal Productivity (DMP)

Diminishing Marginal Productivity (DMP)

A short run production concept where increases in the variable factor of production lead to less and less additional output.

Diminishing Marginal Utility (DMU)

Diminishing Marginal Utility (DMU)

An economic concept that refers to the notion that additional units consumed of a particular commodity provide less and less additional satisfaction relative to previous units consumed.

Dominant Strategy

Dominant Strategy

A game theoretic outcome where the choice of one player is the same independent of choices made by other players in the game.

Economic Agent

Economic Agent

A decision maker involved in any type of economic activity.

Economics

Economics

The study of how a given society allocates scarce resources to meet the unlimited wants and need of its members.

Edgeworth Box

Edgeworth Box

An analytical tool used to study the behavior of two economic agents based on preferences for goods and services when production of those goods is held constant.

Efficiency

Efficiency

A situation in the allocation of resources where the benefits of consuming one more unit exactly equal the (social and private) costs or producing that good.

Equilibrium

Equilibrium

A condition where there is no tendency for an economic variable to change.

Expenditure

Expenditure

The amount spent by a consumer on a bundle of goods or services (the product of market price and quantity demanded).

Factors of Production

Factors of Production

An exhaustive list of inputs required for any type of production.

Factor Prices

Factor Prices

The payments made to the factors of production (rents, wages, interest, and profits).

Final Goods and Services

Final Goods and Services

Goods and services that are purchased for direct consumption.

Fixed Costs of Production

Fixed Costs of Production

Those costs of production that are independent of production levels in the short run.

Flow Variable

Flow Variable

A variable that is measured per unit of time.

Game Theory

Game Theory

A modeling technique that accounts for strategic behavior of economic agents reacting to the actions of others.

Income Effect

Income Effect

A reaction of consumer's demand for goods or services due to changes in purchasing power holding relative prices constant (see Substitution Effect).

Income Elasticity of Demand

Income Elasticity of Demand

A measure of sensitivity of quantity demanded to changes in consumer income.

Income-Neutral Good

Income-Neutral Good

A good where quantity demanded is unchanged when consumer income changes.

Increasing Returns to Scale (IRS)

Increasing Returns to Scale (IRS)

A long run production concept where a doubling of all factor inputs more than doubles the amount of output.

Indifference Curve

Indifference Curve

A set of points that represent different bundles of goods which provide the consumer with the same level of satisfaction (or utility).

Inferior Good

Inferior Good

A good where quantity demanded decreases when consumer income increases (there is an inverse relationship between quantity demanded and income).

Intermediate Goods and Services

Intermediate Goods and Services

Goods (or services) used to produce other goods (i.e., capital equipment).

Lexicographic Preferences

Lexicographic Preferences

Preferences that can be strictly ranked --usually applies in situations where only one good in a bundle is preferred by the consumer.

Long Run Production

Long Run Production

Production activity where all factors of production may vary in quantity. The firm has the freedom to substitute among these factors or production in attempts to minimize costs.

Marginal Rate of Substitution

Marginal Rate of Substitution

The rate by which a consumer may substitute a quantity of one good for another holding his/her level of utility constant.

Marginal Costs

Marginal Costs

The cost of producing one more unit of a good in the short run. A measure of the opportunity costs of the variable inputs in their next best use.

Marginal Revenue

Marginal Revenue

The revenue generated to a firm by selling one more unit of a good or service.

Marginal Utility

Marginal Utility

The satisfaction a consumer receives by consuming one more unit of some good or service.

Market

Market

A place or institution where buyers and sellers come together and exchange factor inputs or final goods and services. A market is one particular type of economic rationing system.

Monopolistic Competition

Monopolistic Competition

A market structure similar to perfect competition in that there are a large number of firms competing in a given industry. However, each firm is selling a differentiated product and may exploit brand preferences such that is may act as a monopolist with respect to its own customers.

Monopoly

Monopoly

A market structure where only one firm exists in a given industry. This firm has a high degree of market power such that it is able to act as a price-maker with respect to market prices.

Needs

Needs

Goods and services essential for human survival.

Negotiation Space

Negotiation Space

A set of consumption bundles (points) relative to an initial or current endowment where one or all consumers can be made better off through trade without harming any other consumers.

Normal Good

Normal Good

A good where quantity demanded increases when consumer income increases (a direct relationship between quantity demanded and income).

Oligopoly

Oligopoly

A market structure with only a few firms in a given industry.

Opportunity Cost

Opportunity Cost

The value of a resource applied to its next best use.

Pareto Improvement

Pareto Improvement

A situation in exchange where one consumer is made better off by a trade without harming the other consumer.

Pareto Optimum

Pareto Optimum

A situation where it is not possible to exchange goods or services without harming one of the agents involved.

Perfect Competition

Perfect Competition

A market structure where many firms exist, each with a small percentage of market share selling a homogeneous product. These firms are all price-takers with no influence on market price.

Price Elastic Demand

Price Elastic Demand

When the percentage change in quantity demanded exceeds the percentage change in market price.

Price Elasticity of Demand

Price Elasticity of Demand

A measure of sensitivity of quantity demanded to changes in market price.

Price Inelastic Demand

Price Inelastic Demand

When the percentage change in quantity demanded is less than the percentage change in market price.

Unitary-elastic Demand

Unitary-elastic Demand

When the percentage change in quantity demanded is exactly equal to the percentage change in market price.

Producer (business firm)

Producer (business firm)

An economic agent that converts inputs (factors of production) into output (goods and services) with the goal of maximizing profits from production and sale of those goods and services.

Producer Optimum

Producer Optimum

A choice of input combinations or output levels that maximize the profits of a producer taking all prices as a given.

Producer's Surplus

Producer's Surplus

The difference between revenue received and the variable costs of production for each unit of a commodity sold. Represents a contribution to fixed costs and producer profits.

Production Function

Production Function

A technical relationship between a certain level of factor inputs and the corresponding level of output.

Production Possibilities Frontier

Production Possibilities Frontier

A relationship between two types of output defining the tradeoff that exists in allocating resources from production of one good to the other.

Profits

Profits

The difference between sales revenue and the costs of production.

Rationing Systems

Rationing Systems

A process used to match the desire for goods and services with their availability.

Relative Prices

Relative Prices

A ratio of any two prices or one particular price compared to a price index.

Resources

Resources

The raw materials and other factors of production that enter the production process or final goods and services that are desired by economic agents.

Revenue

Revenue

The amount received by a producer from the sale of goods and services (the product of market price and quantity sold).

Risk

Risk

A measure of uncertainty about the value of an asset or the benefits of some economic activity.

Satiation

Satiation

A level of consumption where the consumer is fully satisfied in a given period of time.

Scarcity

Scarcity

A physical or economic condition where the quantity desired of a good or service exceeds the availability of that good or service in the absence of a rationing system.

Shortage

Shortage

A market condition where the quantity demanded of a particular good or service exceed the quantity available.

Short Run Production

Short Run Production

Production activity where only one factor of production may vary in quantity. All other factors of production are fixed in quantity. Substitution among factors is not possible.

Speculation

Speculation

The purchase of a good or asset not intended for final consumption but rather in the expectation of future sale at some higher price.

Stock Variable

Stock Variable

A variable measured at point in time.

Substitution Effect

Substitution Effect

The reaction of a consumer's demand for goods based on changes in relative prices holding purchasing power (or utility) constant (see Income Effect).

Substitute Goods

Substitute Goods

A pair of goods where the quantity demanded of one increases when the price of a related good also increases.

Supply

Supply

A relationship between market price and quantities of goods and services made available for sale in a given period of time.

Surplus

Surplus

A market condition where the quantity supplied of a particular commodity exceeds the quantity demanded

Total Effect

Total Effect

The observed change in quantity demanded due to a price change of one particular good.

Transitive Preferences

Transitive Preferences

A logical pattern of preferences where preference of one good over a second good and preference of the second good over a third good imply preference for the first good compared to the third good.

Unrelated Goods

Unrelated Goods

A pair of goods where the quantity demand of one is unaffected by changes in the price of the other.

Utility

Utility

A measure of the satisfaction received from some type of economic activity (i.e., consumption of goods and services or the sale of factor services).

Variable Costs of Production

Variable Costs of Production

Production costs related to changing quantities of a variable factor of production in the short run.

Wants

Wants

Preferences for goods and services over and above human needs.
